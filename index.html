Welcome to the Observatory

How to get to The Observatory

The Observatory is located on the 4th floor of the Physics Building. Start at the fountain of the science complex and face the 2 domes. Walk to the Arc under the domes and go in the door on the left side. Then go up the stairs to your right and go all the way to the 4th floor. It is okay to open the door at the top of the stairs. Then the observatory door is on your left. A video guide is provided here.

Is The Observatory open?

The observatory is only on clear nights Monday - Thursday. The Observatory will be determined to be open by the technicians and updates will be posted above. To help you predict if the observatory will be open click the link above for a helpful graphic

14 Inch Telescope

The TCNJ Observatory is equiped with a Celestron 14 inch Schmidt–Cassegrain telescope

Rotating dome

The primary function of the dome is to protect the telescope and other equipment from external factors so that we can provide the highest quality observations

Knowledgable Technicians

Our technicians have extensive training and experience in astronomy and observatory operations, and they work closely with our students and faculty to ensure that everyone has a positive and enriching experience at the observatory

Understanding the scale of the universe

    It has been said that astronomy is a humbling and character-building experience. There is perhaps no better demonstration of the folly of human conceits than this distant image of our tiny world. — Carl Sagan

    To help understand the scale of the universe we can use proportionality. The distance between the Earth and the Sun is 1 au (which is astronomical units). There are 63241.1 au in one light year. There are 63360 inches in a mile. If we scale the universe down to the point where the distance between the Earth and the Sun is one inch then one light year would be approximately one mile.
